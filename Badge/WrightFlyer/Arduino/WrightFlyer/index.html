System Operations

Dimensions of the Wright Flyer can be found here on the blueprint.

Power is provided by x3 AAA alkaline batteries providing a nominal 4.5V. This power is then regulated down to 3.3V for the microcontroller and other systems.

Thrust is provided by 2 counter-rotating propellers. The counter-rotating aspect is important to negate any undesired torque. The spinning of the propellers can be turned off without affecting any other operation by selecting the Propeller switch to Off. The Propeller Modes switch has 3 positions:

  • Normal: Connects the motors to the WiFi enabled controller with pulse width modulation for controlled spinning.
  • Backup: Connects the motors to a backup pulse width modulated control that functions regardless of WiFi control or state.
  • Full: In this mode the motors are directly wired to the batteries, even bypassing the voltage regulator, to ensure that the pilot gets all available power desired. Furthermore, in this emergency mode even the Power switch has no effect, although the Propeller switch will still function.

WiFi on this badge is controller by the switch labeled WiFi. When not needed battery power can be saved by selecting this mode to Off. Be advised though, that when selected to Off, others will be unable to hack your motor control via WiFi. 😜
